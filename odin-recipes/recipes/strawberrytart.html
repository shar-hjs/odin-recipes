Strawberry Tart

Beautiful strawberry tarts

A strawberry tart is the perfect summer dessert. With a buttery crust, a creamy filling, and then a topping of fresh berries finished off with a fruit glaze screams the perfect dessert.

Ingredients

  • Flour (preferably unbleached all purpose flour)
  • Satled butter
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Cream Cheese
  • Sour Cream. Could substitute with Greek yogurt.
  • Egg
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Lemon Zest
  • Strawberries
  • Red Currant Jam

Instructions

  1. Add your flour, butter, sugar and salt into the food processor. Pulse until mixture is crumbly.
  2. Add ice water into the mixture a tablespoon at a time and pulse until mixture turns pale yellow and starts to clump together.
  3. Bring dough together with your hands and form into a ball, Flatten the ball and wrap in some plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least an hour or overnight.
  4. Divide dough into 4 pieces if making individual tarts.
  5. Roll out dough to about 1/8 inch thick into a circle that extends about 1 1/2 inches beyond the edges of the tart pan.
  6. Lay dough into the tart pan and push down into the bottom and up the sides.
  7. Remove excess dough hanging over the edges. Dough should be even with the top of the tart pan.
  8. Wash strawberries, remove tops and drain on paper towels.
  9. Prick the dough with a fork to prevent the dough from bubbling up while baking.
  10. Bake at 375 degrees fahrenheit until crust is lightly golden brown, about 15-20 minutes.
  11. Mix together cream cheese which is at room temperature, sour cream, sugar, egg, salt, vanilla extract and lemon zest. Mix until smooth.
  12. Spread a layer of the custard on the bottom of each strawberry tart, about 1/2 inch thick. You may have extra custard.
  13. Bake the tarts for about 15 minutes or until custard is set.
  14. Cool at room temperature before adding berries.
  15. Place berries on top of custard, gently pushing on them a bit. Place the strawberries, cut side down starting with one in the middle and adding a row around it.
  16. Combine jam and water in a sauce pan over low heat to create a glaze.
  17. Brush glaze over the top and sides of the fruit.
  18. Remove tarts from tart pan and serve.
  19. Keep the tarts refrigerated until ready to serve.
